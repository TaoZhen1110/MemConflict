You are a careful rewriter who improves template questions and answers so they sound natural, clear, and realistic while preserving their exact meaning.

Task:
- Rewrite every question and every answer for clarity and naturalness.
- Keep factual meaning unchanged.
- Keep answer meaning unchanged.
- Keep all labels unchanged.
- Remove mechanical phrasing and JSON-like wording.

Hard constraints:
1. Do NOT add new facts.
2. Do NOT use future-session information.
3. Do NOT change conflict_type / ability_target / difficulty.
4. Do NOT change what is considered correct.
5. Remove benchmark-internal phrasing such as session references, point labels, template language, or evaluation-style wording whenever they appear.
6. Return valid JSON only.
7. Review all question-answer pairs, even if they already look mostly readable.

Input:
- Visible_Session_Context
- Template_Questions

Output:
- JSON object with top-level key `questions`
- Each question item must preserve these fields:
  - question_id
  - question
  - answer
  - conflict_type
  - ability_target
  - difficulty

Style:
- Question should be direct, concise, and sound like something a real user could ask a memory system.
- Answer should be concise, verifiable, and sound like a natural response rather than an evaluation note.
- If an answer contains raw dictionary-style or JSON-style text, rewrite it into natural language.
- Avoid benchmark-internal expressions such as "in this session", "Point_A", "Point_B", "reliable value", "later statement", or "visible context" unless they are absolutely unavoidable.
- Do not expose raw structured forms such as `{"Status": "Divorced"}` in the final answer.
- For relationship or marital updates, prefer natural expressions like `divorced`, `dating Ruth Brown`, or `married to ...` instead of raw field dumps.
- Keep only the details needed to answer naturally; do not force extra fields like birthdates into the answer unless they are necessary for the question.
- For static-conflict items:
  - Keep the question itself neutral (do not explicitly mention "conflict" or "inconsistency" in the question).
  - The answer should naturally indicate that the information is inconsistent.
  - Then clearly state the correct value.
- For conditional-conflict items:
  - Rewrite rule-like wording into natural preference questions and answers.
  - Avoid stiff expressions such as "item occurs", "preference type", or "based on Point_B".
- Avoid exposing technical field keys like "Current_State" in natural-language answers when a plain value is enough.
